Broadsides! Ship Combat Bestiary

Nyceria Leviathans

The Isles of Maro are, by most measures, a wonderful place to live. The climate is temperate, the trade winds are reliable, and the sunsets over the Shearwater Passage are genuinely among the finest in the known world. The fact that the ocean is also occasionally on fire with residual mana, that rogue arcane storms materialize without warning and do things to compass needles that compasses were not designed to survive, that the wildlife has been saturated with ambient magic long enough to develop opinions about ships, and that the sea floor contains enough pre-Storm wreckage to keep archaeologists employed and in mortal peril simultaneously — all of this is considered, by the millions who call the Isles home, simply part of the texture of life. They came anyway: tritons and tortles and sea-touched refugees washing in from a Feywild that decided it was done with them, merchants and banker-princes who looked at a mana-haunted ocean and saw an underserved market, pirates who looked at those merchants and reached the same conclusion, and archaeologists who looked at all of the above and somehow thought they were the ones taking the interesting risk. The Merchant Princes will take everything you own if you let them. The Pirate Lords will take everything you own and sink the ship for sport. The leviathans are, arguably, the most straightforward party in Nycerian commerce: they do not negotiate, they do not charge tariffs, and they have never once sent a strongly worded letter. What follows is the compiled knowledge of every sailor who made it back to tell someone about it, organized by how badly the encounter in question tends to go.

All damage values use Broadsides! ship combat scale (x10). A T3 leviathan should threaten multiple well-equipped ships simultaneously.
